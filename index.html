The Problem

Kina are eating our reefs bare

Snapper and crayfish — the natural kina predators — have been fished out of New Zealand's coastal waters. Without them, kina populations exploded and grazed vast stretches of reef down to bare rock. 50% of shallow reef kelp around Little Barrier Island is now barren.

Kelp Helpers is restoring the balance — removing kina, returning kelp, and building a circular economy that makes restoration self-funding.
