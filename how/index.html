The Process

How we turn ocean problems into self-funding solutions

Every Kelp Helpers initiative follows the same four-stage validation model — proven first with KinaKrete, now being repeated across every entity in the portfolio.

1

Identify the Problem

Understand the ecological and economic challenge in depth — what's broken, why it persists, and who's affected.

2

Select the Model

Choose the innovation and business model best placed to relieve the problem at scale, not just patch it.

3

Define & Produce the MVP

Build the minimum viable product or service needed to prove the model works in the real world.

4

Achieve Revenue & Funding

Turn proof-of-concept into a self-sustaining revenue stream that funds the next round of restoration.

Proof of Concept

KinaKrete: from invisible waste to award-winning revenue

Kina shells had no economic value — collecting them cost divers money rather than making it, and the barren crisis itself was out of sight and out of mind for most New Zealanders. KinaKrete solved both problems at once: turning waste shell into concrete and ceramic products that put the story directly into people's hands. The result is no longer a concept — it's an award-winning, revenue-producing business.

🏆 Award-winning 💰 Revenue-producing ♻️ Fully circular
The Solution

A circular economy that heals the ocean

We don't just remove kina — we extract value from them, so the operation can fund itself. Every coaster sold, every tonne of concrete poured, funds the next removal.

Kina Are Removed From Barrens

Trained divers work consented barren areas, collecting excess kina. Each diver removes ~600kg per day.

Waste Becomes Products

Shells and spines go to KiwiKrete (concrete) and Calcara (ceramics). Nothing is wasted — the "problem" becomes the raw material.

Products Generate Revenue

Restoration Coaster Sets, KinaKrete sculptures, ceramic surfaces — each sale directly funds more removals.

Kelp Returns — Naturally

Remove the kina, and kelp comes back on its own within 2 years. No planting needed. The ecosystem heals.

Pingo Maps the Recovery

Autonomous drones track kelp health and generate natural capital data — unlocking biodiversity credit financing at scale.
